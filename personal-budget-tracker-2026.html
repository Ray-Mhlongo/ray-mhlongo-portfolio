Project Case Study

Personal Budget Tracker 2026

A personal finance workbook that turns daily transactions into a clear monthly money report.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Without structured tracking, it is difficult to know where money is going, which categories are overspending, and whether savings targets are being met.

Solution

I designed a workbook that combines income and expenses, groups spending by category, and shows variance against budget limits. The summary panel updates automatically.

What Data I Tracked

  • Income streams: salary, side income, freelance, rental, bonus
  • Expense transactions with date, category, amount, and payment method
  • Category budget limits and actual spending
  • Monthly totals: income, expenses, net balance, savings rate
  • Share-of-spend by category for decision support

What I Did as a Data Analyst

  • Collected and categorized raw transaction entries
  • Standardized category names and removed inconsistent values
  • Built SUMIF-based category totals and variance calculations
  • Calculated savings rate and net monthly position
  • Created visual summaries to explain top spending drivers

Main Metrics

  • 5 income sources tracked
  • 50 expense transactions analyzed
  • 18 spending categories monitored
  • Savings rate for March 2026
  • Largest overspend category by variance

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Financial analysis formulas
  • Variance analysis
  • Data visualization

Business Impact

The model makes personal finance decisions easier by showing exact spending pressure points. It supports practical budget adjustments instead of guesswork.
