Local ISP - Retail Hardware Sales Analysis

The Local ISP Project is a data analytics initiative focused on analyzing the sales and profitability of hardware devices such as routers, modems, and network equipment sold by a local Internet Service Provider (ISP).

Project Overview

The goal is to explore the dataset, calculate key metrics, and visualize trends to support business decision-making. The project demonstrates the use of Excel, Google Sheets, and Power BI dashboards to analyze, visualize, and interpret business data for actionable insights.

Dataset Description

  • Part Name: Name of the device (e.g., Router, Modem)
  • Manufacturer: Brand or vendor
  • Shop: Location where the part is sold
  • Province: Province of the shop
  • Unit Cost (R): Purchase cost of each unit
  • Unit Sale Price (R): Selling price per unit
  • Quantity Sold: Number of units sold
  • Total Cost: Calculated as Unit Cost x Quantity Sold
  • Total Sales: Calculated as Unit Sale Price x Quantity Sold
  • Profit: Calculated as Total Sales - Total Cost
  • Profit %: Calculated as Profit / Total Cost

Analysis Tools and Methods

  • Excel: For calculations, formula demonstration, and basic charts
  • Google Sheets: For interactive display of formulas and live data
  • Power BI: For interactive dashboards showing sales trends, profitability, top-selling products, and regional comparisons

Key Formulas Used

  • Total Cost = Unit Cost x Quantity Sold
  • Total Sales = Unit Sale Price x Quantity Sold
  • Profit = Total Sales - Total Cost
  • Profit % = Profit / Total Cost

Project Purpose

  • Identify high-performing products
  • Calculate profitability per product and overall
  • Compare sales performance across shops and provinces
  • Present findings visually through interactive dashboards
