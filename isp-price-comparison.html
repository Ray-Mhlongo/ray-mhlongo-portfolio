Value analysis for internet plans

This case study compares internet packages by looking beyond the monthly price and measuring how much speed customers receive for what they pay.

Case Study

Problem

Customers often choose an internet plan because it is familiar or has the lowest monthly price. The problem is that the cheapest plan is not always the best value when speed, package type, and cost are compared together.

Analysis approach

  • Collected public package data from different ISP sources.
  • Standardized prices, speeds, plan names, and connection types for fair comparison.
  • Calculated cost per Mbps by dividing monthly price by advertised speed.
  • Compared plans by provider, speed range, and value score to identify stronger options.

Methods used

Excel, Power BI, public data collection, value analysis, and a cost efficiency calculation where cost per Mbps equals monthly price divided by Mbps.

Decision value

The analysis made plan comparison clearer by showing value instead of only price. A customer can see when a higher monthly fee gives better speed for the money and when a cheaper plan may cost more per unit of performance.
