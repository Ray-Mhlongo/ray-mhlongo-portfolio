Project Case Study

Ubuntu Kitchen Restaurant Management

A restaurant analytics workbook that tracks sales, loyalty customers, inventory, payroll, and expenses in one dashboard-ready system.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Restaurants generate many transactions daily, but owners often lack one clear view of revenue, costs, staff spend, and stock risk.

Solution

I built a data-rich management workbook that connects sales, loyalty, payroll, inventory, and expense data so performance can be monitored daily.

What Data I Tracked

  • Sales transactions: item, quantity, price, payment type, staff member
  • Loyalty customers: visits, spend, points, tier
  • Expense records: rent, utilities, maintenance, marketing, supplies
  • Payroll details: gross pay, deductions, net pay
  • Inventory lines with reorder-level monitoring

What I Did as a Data Analyst

  • Prepared structured transaction tables for high-volume sales data
  • Calculated revenue trends and menu gross profit percentages
  • Analyzed payment channel mix and customer loyalty tiers
  • Monitored staff cost as a share of revenue
  • Flagged inventory reorder risk and high-cost leakage areas

Main Metrics

  • 1,972+ sales transactions
  • 50 loyalty customers
  • 65+ expense records
  • Menu item profitability ranking
  • Labor and inventory risk indicators

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Business intelligence reporting
  • Restaurant analytics
  • Data visualization

Business Impact

Management can act faster on low-margin items, rising costs, and inventory pressure. This improves both daily operations and long-term planning.
