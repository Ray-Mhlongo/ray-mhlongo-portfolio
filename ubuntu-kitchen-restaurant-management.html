Project Case Study

Ubuntu Kitchen - Restaurant Operations & Business Intelligence Analytics

A business intelligence system that converts restaurant transactions into operational insights for revenue optimization and cost control.

This project demonstrates how operational data from a restaurant can be structured and analyzed to provide management with clear visibility into sales performance, cost control, and customer behavior.

Using Excel-based data modeling, I developed a system that consolidates sales transactions, loyalty customers, payroll, inventory levels, and operating expenses into a centralized analytics environment.

The objective was to transform daily restaurant activity into actionable business intelligence that supports better operational and financial decision-making.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Restaurants process a large number of transactions every day, yet many small businesses lack a unified system to monitor their financial and operational performance.

Without structured data reporting, restaurant owners struggle to answer important questions such as:

  • Which menu items generate the most profit
  • Which customers contribute the most revenue
  • How labor costs compare to daily sales
  • Whether inventory shortages or waste are affecting margins

When operational data is scattered across different records, it becomes difficult for management to gain a clear overview of business performance.

Solution

I developed a restaurant operations analytics workbook that integrates sales, loyalty programs, payroll, expenses, and inventory data into a single structured system.

The system organizes high-volume transaction data into analytical tables and automatically calculates key performance indicators that help management monitor sales performance, cost structure, and customer activity.

Data Organized and Tracked

  • Sales Transactions: menu item sold, quantity purchased, unit price, payment method, staff member handling the transaction
  • Customer Loyalty Data: customer ID, visit frequency, total spending, loyalty points and tier levels
  • Expense Records: rent and utilities, maintenance costs, marketing expenses, supplies and operational purchases
  • Payroll Data: staff gross salary, deductions, net pay, payroll cost tracking
  • Inventory Management: ingredient stock levels, reorder thresholds, inventory consumption monitoring

Data Analyst Responsibilities

  • Structuring high-volume sales transaction datasets for analysis
  • Calculating revenue trends and menu item profitability
  • Analyzing customer loyalty behavior and spending patterns
  • Monitoring labor costs relative to total sales revenue
  • Identifying inventory risk through reorder-level monitoring
  • Designing summary views and dashboards for management reporting

Key Metrics Tracked

The demonstration dataset includes:

  • 1,972+ sales transactions
  • 50 loyalty customers
  • 65+ operational expense records

Key analytical metrics produced include:

  • Menu item profitability rankings
  • Revenue performance trends
  • Labor cost as a percentage of revenue
  • Customer loyalty spending patterns
  • Inventory reorder risk indicators

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Data modeling and structured tables
  • Business intelligence reporting
  • Operational analytics
  • Data visualization dashboards

Business Impact

By consolidating operational data into a structured analytics system, restaurant management can quickly identify revenue drivers, cost pressures, and operational risks.

The system enables faster decision-making around menu pricing, inventory management, staffing costs, and customer engagement strategies.
