Case Study

Problem

PC hardware prices were difficult to compare because product names, categories, shops, and locations were not consistent. Without standard fields, a buyer could not easily tell whether a price difference came from the product, the shop, or the province.

Analysis approach

  • Cleaned raw pricing records and prepared them for analysis.
  • Standardized categories, shops, provinces, manufacturers, and product groupings.
  • Created a SQL structure so prices could be filtered, grouped, and compared.
  • Compared price ranges to spot lower cost products and large price differences.

Methods used

SQL, MySQL, Power BI, GitHub, data cleaning, category standardization, price grouping, and variance checks using comparisons between minimum, average, and maximum prices.

Decision value

The project shows how clean data changes the buying decision. Once prices are normalized, a buyer can compare shops fairly, identify better priced products, and notice when a category or province has unusually high price variance.
