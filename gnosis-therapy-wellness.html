Project Case Study

GNOSIS Therapy and Wellness Service

A simple clinic management workbook that tracks clients, sessions, appointments, and payments in one place.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

The practice had client records, bookings, and payment information spread across different sheets and manual notes. That made it hard to see no-shows, unpaid balances, and monthly performance quickly.

Solution

I built one end-to-end workbook that connects client onboarding, session logging, appointment status, invoicing, and revenue reporting. Everything updates automatically from structured tables.

What Data I Tracked

  • Client details: unique ID, contact info, insurance status
  • Appointment records: date, therapist, status (completed/booked/no show/cancelled)
  • Session data: service type, fee charged, amount paid, balance due
  • Financial summary: monthly revenue and outstanding balances
  • Client timeline: history view for repeat sessions and progress tracking

What I Did as a Data Analyst

  • Defined clean table structure for clients, sessions, and appointments
  • Cleaned and standardized names, dates, and status fields
  • Used formulas (SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, IFERROR) for revenue and attendance KPIs
  • Built simple dashboard cards for active clients, session volume, and balances
  • Reviewed trends: retention, no-show rate, and payment recovery patterns

Main Metrics

  • 50 active clients in the base dataset
  • 50 sessions logged for analysis
  • 50 appointments tracked with status
  • Appointment attendance and no-show rate
  • Outstanding balance by client and month

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Formula modeling
  • Dashboard design
  • Operational analytics

Business Impact

The clinic can now see workload, missed sessions, and money still owed in seconds. This improves follow-up actions and gives better visibility into business health.
