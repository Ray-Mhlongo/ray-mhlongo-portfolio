Case Study

Problem

Small transport operators often track students, trips, payments, vehicles, and costs in different spreadsheets. This makes it difficult to see which routes are profitable, which students still owe money, and where fuel or trip costs are affecting the business.

Analysis approach

  • Structured records for students, trips, routes, vehicles, payments, income, and expenses.
  • Linked trips to routes and payments so activity could be compared against money received.
  • Outlined profit visibility by comparing trip income with fuel and operating costs.
  • Used alerts to highlight missing trips, unpaid students, and routes that need attention.

Methods used

Structured JSON data, dashboard metrics, route comparison, payment tracking, map based route context, and cost visibility using calculations such as profit per trip equals trip income minus trip cost.

Decision value

The project turns daily transport activity into decision support. An operator can see unpaid accounts, active routes, fuel pressure, trip volume, and profit signals before small issues become bigger business problems.

Data Thinking

Cleaner records

Students, trips, routes, vehicles, payments, income, and expenses are stored in consistent fields so the same information can be filtered, counted, and compared.

Better visibility

The dashboard focuses on daily income, monthly profit, outstanding payments, fuel costs, and route activity because these metrics show both cash flow and operational pressure.

Actionable alerts

The system flags missing trips, unpaid students, and high fuel estimates so the operator can follow up quickly and protect route profitability.
