Project Case Study

Rental Property Manager

A multi-property workbook for tracking tenants, rent collection, maintenance, and operating costs.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Managing several properties manually makes it hard to track occupancy, rental arrears, lease expiries, and maintenance costs in one view.

Solution

I created an integrated property management workbook with linked property, tenant, payment, and maintenance tables. It supports monthly performance and tax-ready reporting.

What Data I Tracked

  • Property and room inventory across multiple sites
  • Tenant profiles with lease dates and deposit details
  • Monthly rent payments with status and method
  • Maintenance jobs with supplier, cost, and completion status
  • Operating expense categories for each property

What I Did as a Data Analyst

  • Structured data model for properties, tenants, payments, and maintenance
  • Created occupancy and arrears calculations
  • Tracked lease expiry pipeline using date logic
  • Benchmarked cost-per-unit and rent performance by property
  • Built summary views for management reporting

Main Metrics

  • 7 properties in portfolio scope
  • 50+ tenant records
  • 50+ payment records
  • Occupancy rate by property
  • Rent arrears and lease-expiry risk

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Operational reporting
  • Financial tracking
  • Property analytics

Business Impact

The workbook gives landlords a clearer operational picture and helps prioritize actions on vacancies, unpaid rent, and maintenance spending.
