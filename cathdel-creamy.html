Case Study

Problem

Cathdel Creamy needed a simple way to understand what was selling, which customers were returning, and which products deserved more attention. Without organized sales records, daily activity could not easily support stock, pricing, or loyalty decisions.

Analysis approach

  • Structured sales records by date, product, customer, quantity, and payment value.
  • Separated once off customers from repeat customers to understand loyalty behavior.
  • Compared product sales to identify which items were driving revenue.
  • Outlined simple metrics such as total sales, average order value, repeat purchases, and product ranking.

Methods used

Sales data planning, customer grouping, product performance analysis, dashboard thinking, and simple calculations such as SUM for revenue and AVERAGE for order value.

Decision value

The analysis shows how raw sales can guide practical choices. The owner can see stronger products, spot returning customers, plan loyalty offers, and focus stock decisions on real buying patterns.
