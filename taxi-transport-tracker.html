Project Case Study

Taxi and Transport Tracker - Transport Profitability Analytics

An operational analytics system designed to measure real profitability in a transport business after platform commissions and operating costs.

This project demonstrates how trip-level operational data from a transport business can be structured and analyzed to evaluate true profitability.

Using Excel-based data modeling, I created a system that records trips, platform commissions, operating costs, and maintenance expenses while calculating net revenue and overall profitability.

The objective was to convert raw trip and expense records into clear operational analytics that help transport operators understand margins, cost drivers, and financial performance.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Taxi and transport operators typically track daily fares but often lack visibility into their true operating profit after accounting for platform commissions, fuel costs, maintenance, insurance, and other running expenses.

Without structured data tracking, it becomes difficult to answer important business questions such as:

  • Which trips or routes are most profitable
  • How platform commissions affect revenue
  • How operating costs impact monthly profitability
  • Whether the business is generating sustainable margins

This lack of financial visibility can make it difficult for operators to manage costs and optimize earnings.

Solution

I designed a transport operations analytics workbook that records trips, adjusts fares for platform commissions, and compares total revenue against operating expenses.

The system organizes all transport activity into structured datasets and calculates key profitability metrics, enabling drivers or fleet operators to monitor business performance and identify cost drivers.

Data Collected and Structured

  • Trip Records: route or destination, distance travelled, fare charged, payment type, platform used (Bolt, Snapscan, cash)
  • Platform Fees: commission rates applied by ride platforms, net fare received after deductions
  • Operating Expenses: fuel costs, insurance, vehicle instalments, toll fees, car wash and cleaning costs
  • Maintenance Records: vehicle service history, odometer readings, maintenance cost tracking, next service schedule
  • Financial Summary: monthly revenue totals, total operating expenses, profit and loss comparison

Data Analyst Responsibilities

  • Structuring trip and cost datasets for time-series operational analysis
  • Calculating net fare per trip after platform commission deductions
  • Categorizing expenses to analyze cost drivers
  • Comparing monthly revenue against total operating costs
  • Building a KPI dashboard to track profitability and operational trends

Key Metrics Tracked

The demonstration dataset includes:

  • 50 recorded trips
  • 50 operating expense entries
  • 25 maintenance records

From this data the system calculates:

  • Net fare per trip after commission
  • Monthly profit or loss
  • Expense distribution by category
  • Vehicle maintenance costs
  • Operational profitability trends

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Financial modeling
  • Data structuring and table design
  • KPI dashboards
  • Transport operations analytics

Business Impact

By consolidating trip revenue and operating expenses into a structured analytics system, transport operators gain a clearer understanding of their actual profitability.

The system helps identify which cost categories are reducing margins and provides insight into revenue performance, enabling better financial and operational decision-making.
