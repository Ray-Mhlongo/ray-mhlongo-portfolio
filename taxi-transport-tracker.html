Project Case Study

Taxi and Transport Tracker

A trip-level transport workbook that shows real profitability after platform commissions and operating costs.

Confidentiality note: this case study uses demo or anonymized data for portfolio purposes.

Problem

Taxi operators often see gross fares but not true profit after commissions, fuel, maintenance, and other running costs.

Solution

I built a transport tracking workbook that logs trips, adjusts net fare by platform fee rules, and compares monthly revenue to expenses.

What Data I Tracked

  • Trip logs: route, distance, fare, payment type, platform used
  • Platform commission rates (Bolt, Snapscan, cash)
  • Operating expenses: fuel, insurance, instalments, tolls, car wash
  • Maintenance records with odometer and next service date
  • Monthly revenue and cost summaries for P&L view

What I Did as a Data Analyst

  • Structured trip and cost data for clean time-series analysis
  • Calculated net fare after commission per trip
  • Grouped expenses by category for cost control insight
  • Produced monthly variance view: revenue vs total cost
  • Built KPI dashboard for profitability and operational performance

Main Metrics

  • 50 trips logged
  • 50 expense records
  • 25 maintenance entries
  • Net fare per trip after commission
  • Monthly profit/loss trend

Tools Used

  • Microsoft Excel
  • Operational finance modeling
  • KPI dashboards
  • Transport analytics

Business Impact

The owner can quickly see which periods are profitable and which cost categories are reducing margins. This supports route and spending decisions.
